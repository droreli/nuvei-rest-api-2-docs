# Glossary

A comprehensive guide to payment industry terminology and Nuvei-specific terms.

## A

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Acquirer" icon="building-columns">
    A financial institution (bank) that processes card payments on behalf of merchants. Also called "acquiring bank" or "merchant bank." Nuvei acts as an acquirer in many regions.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="APM (Alternative Payment Method)" icon="wallet">
    Any payment method other than traditional credit/debit cards. Includes bank transfers (iDEAL, SEPA), digital wallets (PayPal, Apple Pay), Buy Now Pay Later (Klarna), and cash-based methods (OXXO).
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Auth (Authorization)" icon="shield-check">
    A transaction type that reserves funds on a cardholder's account without capturing them. The merchant can later settle (capture) the full or partial amount.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="AVS (Address Verification Service)" icon="map-location">
    A fraud prevention measure that verifies the billing address provided by the customer matches the address on file with the card issuer.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## B

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="BIN (Bank Identification Number)" icon="hashtag">
    The first 6-8 digits of a card number that identify the issuing bank and card type. Example: 411111 identifies Visa cards issued by certain banks.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="BNPL (Buy Now Pay Later)" icon="clock">
    A payment method allowing customers to split purchases into installments, often interest-free. Examples: Klarna, Afterpay, Affirm.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## C

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Capture" icon="hand-holding-dollar">
    The process of collecting funds from an authorized transaction. Also called "settlement."
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Card Not Present (CNP)" icon="globe">
    A transaction where the physical card is not present, typically e-commerce or phone orders. Higher fraud risk than Card Present transactions.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Chargeback" icon="rotate-left">
    A transaction reversal initiated by the cardholder's bank, typically due to fraud claims or disputes. Merchants may lose the transaction amount plus fees.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Checksum" icon="shield">
    A hash value calculated from request parameters and a secret key. Used by Nuvei REST API 1.0 to verify request authenticity.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="CIT (Customer-Initiated Transaction)" icon="user">
    A transaction initiated by the customer in real-time, typically requiring 3DS authentication.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="CVV/CVC" icon="lock">
    Card Verification Value/Code - the 3-4 digit security code on the back (or front for Amex) of a card.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## D

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="DMN (Direct Merchant Notification)" icon="bell">
    Nuvei's webhook system that sends real-time notifications to merchants when payment events occur. Also called IPN or callback.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Dynamic Descriptor" icon="tag">
    The merchant name and details that appear on customer bank statements. Can be customized per transaction.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## E

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="EMV" icon="microchip">
    Europay, Mastercard, Visa - the global standard for chip-based card payments. Named after the three companies that developed it.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## F

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Frictionless Flow" icon="bolt">
    A 3DS 2.0 authentication flow where the customer is authenticated without a challenge, based on risk analysis. Results in a seamless checkout experience.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## G

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Gateway" icon="door-open">
    A service that securely transmits payment data between merchant and processor/acquirer. Nuvei provides gateway services.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## I

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Issuer" icon="credit-card">
    The bank that issued the payment card to the customer. Responsible for approving/declining transactions.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## L

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Liability Shift" icon="scale-balanced">
    When fraud liability transfers from merchant to issuer upon successful 3DS authentication.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## M

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="MCC (Merchant Category Code)" icon="store">
    A 4-digit code assigned to businesses based on their primary goods or services. Affects interchange rates and card restrictions.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="MIT (Merchant-Initiated Transaction)" icon="server">
    A transaction initiated by the merchant without customer interaction, such as recurring payments or delayed charges. Often exempt from SCA.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="MOR (Merchant of Record)" icon="receipt">
    The entity responsible for processing payments and appearing on customer statements. Takes on financial responsibility for transactions.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## N

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Network Token" icon="key">
    A card number replacement provided by card networks (Visa, Mastercard) for secure recurring transactions. Automatically updates when cards are reissued.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## O

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="OCT (Original Credit Transaction)" icon="arrow-right">
    A transaction that pushes funds to a card (payout) rather than pulling funds. Used for refunds to different cards, marketplace payouts, etc.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## P

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="PAN (Primary Account Number)" icon="hashtag">
    The full card number (typically 16 digits) embossed on a payment card.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Payout" icon="money-bill-transfer">
    Sending funds to a customer via their card or bank account. Opposite of a payment/deposit.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="PCI DSS" icon="shield-halved">
    Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard - security requirements for handling card data. Compliance level depends on transaction volume and card data handling.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="PSD2" icon="landmark">
    Payment Services Directive 2 - European regulation requiring Strong Customer Authentication (SCA) for electronic payments.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## R

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Recurring Payment" icon="repeat">
    A scheduled, repeat transaction charged to a customer at regular intervals (subscriptions).
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Refund" icon="rotate-left">
    Returning funds to a customer after a successful transaction. Can be full or partial.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## S

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Sale" icon="shopping-cart">
    A transaction type that authorizes and captures funds in a single request.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="SCA (Strong Customer Authentication)" icon="user-shield">
    PSD2 requirement for two-factor authentication using: something you know (PIN), something you have (phone), or something you are (biometrics).
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Session Token" icon="ticket">
    A temporary token from Nuvei's `/openOrder` endpoint used to authenticate payment requests.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Settlement" icon="building-columns">
    The process of transferring funds from the customer's bank to the merchant's bank. Also refers to capturing an authorized transaction.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Simply Connect" icon="code">
    Nuvei's embedded checkout solution that provides a customizable payment form within your site.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## T

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="3D Secure (3DS)" icon="shield">
    A security protocol for authenticating online card transactions. Version 2.0 supports frictionless authentication and is required for PSD2/SCA compliance.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Tokenization" icon="key">
    Replacing sensitive card data with a non-sensitive token. Enables recurring payments without storing actual card numbers.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Transaction ID" icon="fingerprint">
    Unique identifier assigned by Nuvei to each transaction. Used for lookups, refunds, and support inquiries.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## U

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="UPO (User Payment Option)" icon="wallet">
    Nuvei's term for a saved payment method (tokenized card or APM) associated with a user. Identified by `userPaymentOptionId`.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## V

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Void" icon="ban">
    Canceling an authorized transaction before settlement. No funds are captured.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## W

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Webhook" icon="bell">
    Server-to-server HTTP notification sent when events occur. Also called DMN, IPN, or callback.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Web SDK" icon="code">
    Nuvei's JavaScript library for building custom payment forms while maintaining PCI compliance by handling card data securely.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Nuvei-Specific Terms

| Term | Description |
|------|-------------|
| `merchantId` | Your unique Nuvei merchant identifier |
| `merchantSiteId` | Identifier for a specific website/app under your merchant account |
| `clientRequestId` | Your unique identifier for each API request |
| `clientUniqueId` | Your unique identifier for each transaction (order) |
| `userTokenId` | Identifier for a customer in your system |
| `userPaymentOptionId` | ID of a saved payment method (UPO) |
| `transactionId` | Nuvei's unique transaction identifier |
| `authCode` | Authorization code from the issuer |
| `sessionToken` | Temporary authentication token from /openOrder |
